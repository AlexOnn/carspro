Top Auction Platforms in Europe

When you're looking for a great deal on a vehicle or sourcing in bulk for a dealership, European car auctions become a key option. Here’s our practical breakdown of three major platforms used by professionals: Autobid.de, Autorola, and Auto1. This overview is based on our experience — not promotional claims — to help you understand how these auctions work for real.

1. Autobid.de

A well-established German auction with physical and online bidding. Locations across Germany (Munich, Hamburg, Berlin, etc.). Cars typically come with detailed inspection reports. Commission ranges from 2.7% to 3.5% (min. €500). If claiming VAT refund, logistics must be handled by an approved transport company registered with the auction.

2. Autorola

Fully online auction platform with cars coming from lease companies across the EU. Listings may have fixed prices or be auction-based. Commission typically €500–1000. Private buyers cannot participate directly — a broker is needed. Vehicle condition reports vary in quality. Logistics are more flexible than other platforms.

3. Auto1

Offers both bidding and fixed-price models. Vehicles usually come from private sellers via a related car-buying company. Cars are often sold as-is with limited documentation. Pickup requires scheduling, and daily storage fees apply after deadlines (€25–35/day). Prices may be attractive, but hidden issues are common.
